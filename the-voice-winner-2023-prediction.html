Who Will Win The Voice Season 24?

Publish date: 2024-08-07

The Voice coaches may have already found this season’s winner! Based on an interview in the final battle rounds, we may have a clue about who will win The Voice Season 24. Learn which battle earned a standing ovation from the panel of judges, below.

The Voice is NBC’s reality TV singing competition where four celebrity coaches form teams of contestants who compete against each other until there’s one winner. Part of The Voice franchise, the show premiered in April 2011 and aims to find unsighted singing talent. Winners receive $100,000 and a record deal with University Music Group. The show is currently in its 24th season and features Reba McEntire, Niall Horan, Gwen Stefani and John Legend as judges.

We’ve reached the final battle rounds of The Voice Season 24, and the competition is heating up. Entertainment Tonight revealed an exclusive look at the final performances — and the coaches think this musician could win it all.

Who will win The Voice Season 24?

Who will win The Voice Season 24? We’re not sure yet, but one of the judges hinted that Huntley Lenderman could win The Voice. Team Niall’s Huntley and Brailey Lenderman sang an electric rendition of Hootie & the Blowfish’s “Hold My Hand.” The battle round performance got all four judges out of their seats.

“I think y’all did a wonderful job,” McEntire told the brother-sister duo. “It kind of made me a little envious that I didn’t get to be up there singing with you.” However, McEntire signaled Huntley out as a “force of nature on stage,” a sentiment that continued with all of the coaches.

“Brailey, you were powerful,” Legend also said in the clip. “The problem is, you were against Huntley. The magnetism and the energy that you convey is just very attractive and it connects people to you… Dude, you’re great.”

“I was just not expecting that!” Stefani said, adding “Huntley, I’m worried that you might win this show… Your precision and perfection of how you did, it’s hard to believe.”

Finally, the siblings’ coach Niall Horan praised Huntley’s stage presence. “It’s just insane to watch you prowl around that stage while singing that well.” He also told Brailey that she was “absolutely on fire up there.”

Huntley shared the performance on Instagram, and fans flooded the singer’s comments with acclaim. “Huntley is hands down the BEST not only this season but The Voice history to date in my opinion,” one user wrote. Meanwhile, another fan suggested that he should leave the competition and receive a record deal right away. “They need to just take you out of the competition and give you a record deal… your voice is amazing and you deserve this.”

For the 33-year-old’s blind audition, he sang “She Talks to Angels” by The Black Crowes. “You sing your own way. You sing very direct, and [with] so much power,” Horan said to Huntley. “The control that you have, it’s so beautiful to listen to. You made us feel, and that’s a really important thing.”
