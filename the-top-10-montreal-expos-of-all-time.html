The Top 10 Montreal Expos of All-Time

Publish date: 2024-08-26

The Montreal Expos, a team that later became the Washington Nationals, have had a rich history with several remarkable players who left an indelible mark on the franchise.

1. Andre Dawson

Andre Dawson, also known as "The Hawk," is undeniably one of the most iconic players in the history of the Montreal Expos.

The outfielder spent 11 years with the Expos, where he not only racked up an impressive 224 home runs but also exhibited exceptional defensive skills that earned him multiple Gold Glove awards.

Despite battling knee issues towards the end of his tenure, Dawson's prowess in both center and right field significantly contributed to the team's success during his time with the Expos.

His remarkable achievements and unwavering dedication to the franchise firmly establish him as one of the all-time greats in Expos history.

2. Tim Raines

Tim Raines, a switch-hitting outfielder, was a force to be reckoned with during his tenure with the Montreal Expos.

Raines' exceptional two-way abilities, highlighted by his impressive batting average, on-base percentage, and a franchise-record 635 stolen bases, solidified his status as one of the best players in the National League during the 1980s.

3. Gary Carter

Gary Carter, a prolific catcher, made an indelible impact during his 10-year stint with the Montreal Expos.

Renowned for his powerful hitting and solid defensive skills, Carter's 220 home runs and six All-Star appearances firmly established him as a cornerstone of the Expos' lineup.

His exceptional contributions behind the plate significantly influenced the team's performance, ultimately earning him a well-deserved induction into the Hall of Fame in 2003.

4. Vladimir Guerrero

Vladimir Guerrero's tenure with the Montreal Expos was nothing short of spectacular.

Known for his exceptional batting prowess and outstanding outfield arm, Guerrero terrorized opposing pitchers with his remarkable .323/.390/.588 batting line during his eight seasons with the Expos.

His four All-Star selections as an Expo and his ability to consistently deliver exceptional offensive performances cemented his status as one of the most electrifying players in Expos history.

5. Steve Rogers

As the fourth overall pick in the 1971 draft, Steve Rogers evolved into one of the most distinguished pitchers in the history of the Montreal Expos.

With 158 victories and an impressive 3.17 ERA in 13 seasons with the club, Rogers' consistency and durability on the mound were instrumental in shaping the Expos' pitching staff.

6. Tim Wallach

Tim Wallach, the Expos' all-time leader in games played with 1,767, spent 13 seasons with the team, 11 of which were at the hot corner.

His exceptional defensive skills and offensive prowess, highlighted by his three Gold Gloves, five All-Star selections, and a fourth-place finish in the MVP voting in 1987, underscore his immense contribution to the Expos' success.

Wallach's impact as a cornerstone of the team's infield and his remarkable consistency further solidify his place among the Expos' all-time greats.

7. Rusty Staub

Despite his relatively short stint with the Montreal Expos, Rusty Staub's impact was profound.

His dedication to the team's success, demonstrated through his remarkable offensive performances, three All-Star appearances, and his efforts to positively impact the community, firmly established him as a beloved figure in Expos history.

The team's decision to retire Staub's number 10 speaks volumes about his enduring legacy within the franchise.

8. Jose Vidro

Jose Vidro's stellar eight-year tenure with the Expos, during which he achieved a .300 or better batting average for five consecutive seasons, underscores his exceptional offensive capabilities.

Vidro's three All-Star selections and a Silver Slugger award further exemplify his pivotal role as a key contributor to the team's lineup. 

9. Pedro Martinez

Pedro Martinez's remarkable performances as a pitcher during his tenure with the Montreal Expos highlighted his undeniable talent and impact on the team's success.

His notable achievements, including multiple All-Star selections and his exceptional pitching prowess, firmly establish him as one of the top pitchers in Expos history. 

10. Javier Vazquez

Javier Vazquez's tenure as the top starting pitcher in the Expos rotation for six seasons underscored his pivotal role in shaping the team's pitching staff.

His consistent performances on the mound, exemplified by his 64 wins and more than 200 innings pitched for four straight years, firmly establish him as a key contributor to the Expos' success. 

 

The top 10 players highlighted in this article have not only shaped the team's success but have also etched their names in the annals of Expos history, embodying the spirit and excellence of the franchise.
