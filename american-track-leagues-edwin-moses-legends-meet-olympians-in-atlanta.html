American Track League's Edwin Moses Legends Meet: Olympians In Atlanta

Publish date: 2024-05-15

Get ready to witness track and field history in the making at the upcoming Edwin Moses Legends Meet, hosted by the American Track League in Atlanta, Georgia on May 31st. This event promises an electrifying showcase of athletic prowess, featuring a star-studded lineup of legendary athletes and rising stars. From Olympic champions to promising young talents, the meet is a celebration of athleticism, skill, and sportsmanship that is not to be missed.

Meet the Legends: A Lineup Like No Other

Legends Who Shaped Track and Field

Veronica Campbell Brown: A Jamaican sprint queen with an impressive collection of 8 Olympic medals, including 3 golds. Her lightning speed and unwavering determination have solidified her status as one of the most celebrated female sprinters in history.

Edwin Moses: The name behind the meet, a true icon of track and field, with two Olympic gold medals and an unprecedented dominance in the 400m hurdles that inspired athletes worldwide.

Allen Johnson: Hurdling maestro who captured Olympic gold and secured his legacy as one of the greatest hurdlers of all time.

Dwight Phillips: Long jump champion with multiple world championship titles and an Olympic gold medal, leaving an indelible imprint on the sport.

Terrence Trammell: A formidable force in the 110m hurdles and the 4x100m relay, boasting two Olympic silver medals and a reputation as a fierce competitor on the track.

Randall Cunningham: NFL legend, bringing his versatile athleticism to the meet, promising a spectacle like no other.

Future Olympic Stars Ready to Shine

In addition to these revered track legends, the event will feature a lineup of promising young athletes who are poised to make their mark on the track and field world:

  • Ronnie Baker (100m)
  • Sydney McLaughlin (400mH)
  • Keni Harrison (100mH)
  • Tamari Davis (200m)
  • Jasmine Camacho-Quinn (100mh)
  • Elijah Hall (100m)
  • Christina Clemons (100mH)
  • Alexis Holmes (400m)
  • Matthew Boling (200m)

These incredible athletes represent the future of track and field, and their participation adds an exciting dynamic to the meet, showcasing the talent and potential that will drive the sport forward.
